Elementary School Initiatives

CodeVA invites Virginia elementary school teachers to become a part of the effort to bring STEM literacy -- Computer Science -- to our students. CodeVA, a nonprofit 501c3 partnered with Code.org, is making available to all Virginia elementary school teachers a professional development opportunity designed to help you integrate exciting and powerful computer science/computational thinking lessons into your classroom.

Upcoming Events

These sessions are open to all K5 educator (public and private)!

  • Saturday- November 5, 2016 in Richmond, VA - Register here!

Contact Us To Schedule A School Or District - Wide Training

Our training is funded in partnership with Code.org and includes all materials included a printed copy of the K5 Curriculum and access to the online learning environment.

This elementary school curriculum allows even the youngest students to explore the limitless world of computing- at no cost for schools. The courses blend online, self-guided and self-paced tutorials with “unplugged”activities that require no computer at all. Each course consists of about 20 lessons that may be implemented as one unit or over the course of a semester. Even kindergarten-aged pre-readers can participate.

Participating teachers receive a printed copy of the Code.org K-5 curriculum guide, which includes detailed lesson plans and worksheets to use with your lessons, along with a chart correlating each lesson to the Virginia Standards of Learning.

Computer Science in Math

  • Bootstrap World
  • Code.org

CS in Algebra teaches algebraic and geometric concepts through computer programming. The nine units focus on concepts like order of operations, the Cartesian plane, function composition and definition, and solving word problems - all within the context of video game design. By shifting classwork from abstract pencil-and-paper problems to a series of relevant programming problems, CS in Algebra demonstrates how algebra applies in the real world, using an exciting, hands-on approach to create something cool.

Dates: Jul 18 - 20, 2016

Location: Richmond,VA
